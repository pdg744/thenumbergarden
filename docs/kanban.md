# The Number Garden Kanban

This board is the working project-management document for The Number Garden.

It is intentionally simple. The goal is to help a solo founder keep momentum without building a fake enterprise process.

## How to use this board

- Move items between columns as work progresses.
- Keep `In Progress` to 1 to 3 items max.
- Break large vague tasks into concrete deliverables.
- Prefer tasks that end in a visible asset, decision, or conversation.
- Review the board at least twice per week.

A task is done when one of these is true:

## Focus areas

### 1. Site and messaging

Goal:
Make the site convert interest into conversations.

Typical tasks:

- homepage clarity
- offerings clarity
- contact flow
- CTA hierarchy
- practical trust signals

### 2. Outreach and enrollment

Goal:
Generate the first real family conversations.

Typical tasks:

- announcement copy
- outreach scripts
- intake questions
- follow-up system
- inquiry tracking

### 3. Social content and publishing

Goal:
Make regular Meta publishing simple enough to sustain without losing editorial control.

Operating model:

- Planning lives in Markdown content-calendar files.
- Assets stay in their natural source/generated/export locations.
- Publishing packets gather approved captions, links, platform notes, and selected assets.
- Automation bridges, such as Make.com, should read approved handoff artifacts later.
- Actual publishing remains manual until the approval workflow is proven.

Typical tasks:

- monthly calendar pages
- campaign briefs
- caption drafts
- asset selection
- publish packets
- Make.com handoff notes

### 4. Studio setup

Goal:
Make the physical and operational opening feel calm and credible.

Typical tasks:

- room readiness
- signage
- initial schedule
- policies
- payment and onboarding flow

## Weekly rhythm

Suggested rhythm for maintaining this board:

- Monday: choose 1 to 3 `In Progress` items
- Midweek: update status and note blockers
- Friday: move completed items and choose the next focus
