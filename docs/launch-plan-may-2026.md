# Launch Plan: April 14 to May 31, 2026

This is a practical path forward for The Number Garden given one key reality:

- the lease begins on May 1, 2026
- as of April 14, 2026, there has been no publicity or outreach yet

That is not ideal, but it is still very workable if we optimize for a focused soft launch rather than pretending we are doing a full-scale public opening.

## Strategic stance

The goal is not:

- to fill the studio instantly
- to build a giant marketing machine
- to perfect every page, policy, and offering before anyone hears about it

The goal is:

- to generate the first wave of real conversations
- to identify the first 5 to 15 genuinely aligned families
- to learn quickly from actual inquiries
- to begin occupancy and programming without chaos

The right near-term framing is `soft launch`, not `grand opening`.

## Core constraints

Right now, the founder is effectively carrying four jobs:

- pedagogy
- enrollment
- operations
- local outreach

That means the launch plan has to be simple enough to survive real life. If a plan only works under ideal discipline, it is the wrong plan.

## Priority order

For the next six weeks, priorities should be:

1. Make it easy for interested families to understand the offer and contact you.
2. Start local outreach immediately, even before the space is fully ready.
3. Convert outreach into conversations, not into polished marketing assets.
4. Use early conversations to refine pricing, scheduling, and fit language.
5. Treat the first cohort as a pilot community, not as a finished institution.

## What matters most before May 1

Before the lease begins, the business does not need everything.

It does need:

- a clear invitation
- a short explanation of who this is for
- a simple inquiry path
- a small outreach list
- a repeatable way to reply to interested families

It does not yet need:

- a fully built classroom
- complex automation
- elaborate branding campaigns
- a broad menu of programs
- perfect certainty about every policy

## Recommended launch model

Start with a `founding families soft launch`.

This framing helps because it:

- makes early participation feel special rather than unfinished
- gives you permission to iterate
- creates urgency without hype
- fits the intimate, relationship-based nature of the studio

Suggested language:

"The Number Garden is beginning with a small founding group of families as the studio takes root in Portland."

## Timeline

## Phase 1: Immediate setup

### April 14 to April 20

Objective: make the business contactable and talk-about-able.

By the end of this phase, someone who hears about The Number Garden should be able to:

- understand the basic vision
- see that it is real and local
- know whether they might be a fit
- contact you without friction

Critical tasks:

- tighten homepage and offerings copy around fit and next step
- create a short founding families message
- define the first inquiry workflow
- make a small outreach list of local humans and institutions
- begin direct outreach immediately

Deliverables:

- one-paragraph "what this is" summary
- one-paragraph "who this is for" summary
- one founding families invitation
- one intake email template
- one follow-up email template
- one outreach tracker

## Phase 2: Pre-lease outreach sprint

### April 21 to April 30

Objective: start conversations before the doors officially open.

This phase is about momentum, not polish.

Targets for this phase:

- 20 to 40 warm or semi-warm outreach touches
- 8 to 15 genuine responses
- 5 to 10 parent conversations
- 2 to 6 strong prospective fits

Channels to prioritize:

- personal network
- local parent communities
- values-aligned schools and microschools
- homeschool circles
- educators who know families personally
- friendly one-to-one introductions

Channels to deprioritize for now:

- broad paid advertising
- elaborate social media strategy
- large public events
- highly produced launch materials

## Phase 3: Soft opening month

### May 1 to May 31

Objective: turn interest into a calm, credible beginning.

During this phase, the studio should focus on:

- inquiry conversations
- intro sessions
- room readiness
- a small number of early programming rhythms
- collecting real signals about demand and fit

Success in May is not "fully booked."

Success in May is:

- the first families enrolling
- the first students having strong experiences
- a clearer sense of scheduling demand
- better language for the next wave of outreach

## Weekly operating rhythm

For a one-person launch, use a simple weekly rhythm:

### Monday

- outreach
- follow-ups
- schedule conversations

### Tuesday

- parent conversations
- copy refinement based on objections and questions

### Wednesday

- space setup
- pedagogy planning
- session design

### Thursday

- more outreach
- more conversations
- local relationship-building

### Friday

- admin
- tracker review
- next-week priorities

This rhythm matters because it prevents outreach from being endlessly postponed by the more emotionally satisfying work of pedagogy and room design.

## Minimum viable outreach strategy

You do not need "publicity" in the abstract. You need real people hearing a credible invitation.

Start with four outreach lanes.

### 1. Personal network announcement

Write one concise message to:

- friends
- former colleagues
- parents you know
- educators
- mathematically inclined acquaintances

Goal:

- ask for intros, not just applause

Best angle:

- "I’m opening a joyful, inquiry-driven math studio in Portland and looking to connect with a small founding group of aligned families."

### 2. Local connector outreach

Reach out personally to people who already have trust with families.

Examples:

- teachers
- school administrators
- tutors
- homeschool organizers
- enrichment providers
- neighborhood community organizers

Goal:

- ask for a short conversation or referral, not a giant partnership

### 3. Parent community presence

Show up in a few places where local families already gather.

Examples:

- neighborhood parent groups
- homeschool groups
- local listservs
- community boards
- family-oriented online groups

Goal:

- post a warm, specific invitation

### 4. Soft in-person visibility

Once the lease starts, basic physical visibility matters.

Examples:

- window signage
- printed cards or flyers
- a simple "opening soon" message
- a small open house or conversation hour later in May

Goal:

- make the project legible to the neighborhood

## Recommended messaging focus

Early messaging should answer three questions fast:

1. What is this?
2. Who is it for?
3. What should I do next?

Suggested positioning structure:

- The Number Garden is a joyful, inquiry-driven math studio in Portland.
- It is for families seeking deeper challenge, warmer support, or a more alive mathematical culture than school usually offers.
- Founding-family conversations are now open.

## The likely early audience

Do not market to everyone.

The most promising early audiences are probably:

- parents of bright but underchallenged kids
- parents of kids with a strained relationship to school math
- homeschool and alternative-education families
- educators who already believe in inquiry-based learning

That audience matches the current brand and is more likely to respond to thoughtful copy than to generic enrichment language.

## Founding families offer

A founding families frame is useful if it includes real meaning.

Possible components:

- first access to intro sessions
- early input on scheduling
- small initial cohort size
- a more personal relationship with the founder
- pricing or membership terms that are simple and stable for the first wave

This should not feel gimmicky. It should feel honest: early families are helping shape the culture.

## Immediate decisions to make

These do not all need final answers today, but they should be clarified quickly.

### 1. What exactly are you inviting people into first?

Choose the near-term call to action:

- book an intro conversation
- join the founding families list
- attend an open house
- schedule an intro session

Recommendation:

- use `book an intro conversation` or `reach out about fit` first

That is the lightest-friction next step.

### 2. What age range is most realistic at launch?

Recommendation:

- choose a narrower practical launch band even if the philosophy is broad

For example:

- early elementary and upper elementary
- or upper elementary through middle school

A narrower starting band makes messaging, curriculum, and scheduling easier.

### 3. What is the first real format?

Recommendation:

- start with intro sessions plus one or two recurring membership rhythms

Too many formats create complexity before demand is understood.

### 4. What kind of demand signal are you collecting?

Track at minimum:

- who reached out
- where they came from
- age of student
- challenge/support need
- level of interest
- next step

## Numbers that would count as progress

A solo founder can underestimate how much progress small numbers represent.

By May 31, 2026, these would be meaningful signs of traction:

- 30 to 50 total targeted outreach touches
- 10 to 20 real inquiry replies
- 6 to 12 conversations
- 3 to 8 intro sessions
- 2 to 5 enrolled families

Those are not huge numbers, but for a local relationship-based studio, they would be real momentum.

## Biggest risks right now

### Risk 1. Waiting to outreach until everything feels ready

This is the main danger.

If outreach starts only after the space feels complete, May will become a setup month with no enrollment pipeline behind it.

### Risk 2. Hiding behind philosophy

The site already has strong vision. The next challenge is translation.

Families need to understand:

- whether this is for their child
- how the process works
- what kind of support or challenge is available

### Risk 3. Overbuilding offers before hearing from real families

The current offerings are promising, but actual inquiry patterns should shape what stabilizes first.

### Risk 4. Trying to launch at full scale emotionally

The first month should be treated as a soft opening and learning period, not as a referendum on the whole venture.

## Recommended founder mindset

For the next 45 days, the job is not to prove that The Number Garden is already a mature institution.

The job is to:

- make it real
- make it legible
- make it easy to respond
- start the first trusting relationships

That is enough.
