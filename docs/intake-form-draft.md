# Intake Form Strategy

The Number Garden should use two separate forms.

That structure fits the current stage of the business much better than trying to make one form do everything.

## Why two forms

There are really two different jobs here:

1. A low-friction way for interested families to raise their hand
2. A deeper way to get to know a family once there is real mutual interest

If those jobs are combined into one form, the result is usually too long for first contact and too shallow for real intake.

## Recommended system

### Form 1: Interest Form

Purpose:

- make it easy to reach out
- capture enough context for a thoughtful reply
- support outreach and early conversion

Use when:

- a family clicks through from the website
- someone hears about the studio through outreach
- a parent wants to express interest but is not ready for a full intake

Design principle:

- warm
- short
- family-centered
- easy to complete in 2 to 4 minutes

### Form 2: Family Profile

Purpose:

- understand the family and student context in a richer way
- prepare for an intro session or fit conversation
- gather details that would be cumbersome or premature in first contact

Use when:

- a family seems like a plausible fit
- a conversation has been scheduled
- a family is moving toward enrollment

Design principle:

- thoughtful
- personal
- still human and non-bureaucratic
- detailed enough to guide a real conversation

## Form 1: Interest Form

Suggested title:

`The Number Garden: Interest Form`

Suggested description:

`Thank you for your interest in The Number Garden. This short form gives me a first sense of your family and what brings you here. After you submit it, I will follow up by email about next steps.`

## Recommended questions for Form 1

### 1. Parent or caregiver name

- Type: short answer
- Required: yes

### 2. Email address

- Type: short answer
- Required: yes

### 3. Phone number

- Type: short answer
- Required: optional

Suggested help text:

`Optional, but helpful if text is the easiest way to coordinate.`

### 4. What brings your family to The Number Garden right now?

- Type: paragraph
- Required: yes

Suggested prompt text:

`A few sentences is plenty. You might share what is drawing you here, what your family is looking for, or what kind of mathematical experience you hope to find.`

### 5. Which child or children are you thinking about right now?

- Type: paragraph
- Required: yes

Suggested prompt text:

`You can include names, ages, and anything simple that would help me get oriented.`

### 6. What kind of support, challenge, or experience are you hoping for?

- Type: checkboxes
- Required: yes

Suggested choices:

- More challenge
- More confidence and support
- A warmer relationship to math
- Richer mathematical experiences
- A better fit than school math currently provides
- Help rebuilding foundations
- A community for a math-loving child
- I am not quite sure yet

### 7. How did you hear about The Number Garden?

- Type: multiple choice or dropdown
- Required: yes

Suggested choices:

- Friend or family
- Teacher or educator
- Tutor or enrichment provider
- Homeschool community
- Neighborhood or local group
- Social media
- Web search
- Walked by the studio
- Other

### 8. What kind of next step would be most helpful?

- Type: multiple choice
- Required: yes

Suggested choices:

- A short introductory conversation
- Information about upcoming openings
- Help thinking about fit for my family
- Information about scheduling and format

### 9. Anything else you would like to share?

- Type: paragraph
- Required: optional

## Form 1 summary

This form should stay light.

If you want a very lean version, Form 1 can be just these five questions:

1. Parent or caregiver name
2. Email address
3. What brings your family here right now?
4. Which child or children are you thinking about right now?
5. What kind of next step would be most helpful?

## Suggested confirmation message for Form 1

`Thank you for reaching out. I’ll read your note and follow up by email soon. I’m beginning with a small founding group of families and appreciate your interest in The Number Garden.`

## Form 2: Family Profile

Suggested title:

`The Number Garden: Family Profile`

Suggested description:

`Thank you for taking the time to share more about your family. This helps me prepare for a thoughtful conversation and begin imagining what kind of support, challenge, or mathematical experience might be the best fit.`

## Recommended questions for Form 2

This should be sent only after initial interest is established.

### 1. Parent or caregiver name

- Type: short answer
- Required: yes

### 2. Best email for follow-up

- Type: short answer
- Required: yes

### 3. Tell me a little about your family.

- Type: paragraph
- Required: yes

Suggested prompt text:

`Anything that feels relevant is welcome: your family’s educational context, your children, your rhythms, your values, or what has shaped your relationship to learning.`

### 4. Which child or children are you hoping to involve at The Number Garden?

- Type: paragraph
- Required: yes

Suggested prompt text:

`Please include names and ages.`

### 5. How would you describe each child’s current relationship to math?

- Type: paragraph
- Required: yes

Suggested prompt text:

`You might describe confidence, curiosity, frustration, pace, habits, interests, or anything else that feels important.`

### 6. What feels like it is working well right now?

- Type: paragraph
- Required: optional

### 7. What feels difficult, frustrating, or not well served right now?

- Type: paragraph
- Required: yes

### 8. What are you hoping might grow or change?

- Type: paragraph
- Required: yes

### 9. Is there anything about your child or family that would help me welcome you well?

- Type: paragraph
- Required: optional

Suggested prompt text:

`This could include temperament, sensitivities, learning differences, social comfort, pacing, family logistics, or anything else that feels relevant.`

### 10. What kind of scheduling are you most interested in?

- Type: checkboxes
- Required: optional

Suggested choices:

- Weekday afternoons
- Weekday evenings
- Weekends
- Flexible
- Not sure yet

### 11. Is there anything else you would like me to know before we talk?

- Type: paragraph
- Required: optional

## Questions to avoid even in Form 2

I would still avoid:

- testing-heavy intake
- long diagnostic checklists
- institutional special-education language unless truly necessary
- overly rigid multi-child matrices

You want the form to support a relationship, not replace one.

## Recommended site flow

The website should point to Form 1, not Form 2.

The flow should look like this:

1. Family visits the site
2. Family completes the Interest Form
3. Founder reviews responses and follows up
4. If there is likely fit, founder sends the Family Profile or schedules a conversation first
5. More detailed intake happens only after mutual interest is real

## Recommendation

For now:

- build Form 1 first
- embed or link Form 1 on the contact page
- hold Form 2 as a follow-up tool

That gives you a clean public-facing entry point without overcomplicating the first interaction.
