# Launch Plan: Summer 2026

This is the current launch and marketing plan for The Number Garden as of June 3, 2026.

The original April and May launch posture was a focused soft launch: make the studio real, legible, and contactable; start real local conversations; and avoid pretending to be a mature institution before the first families arrived. That stance still matters, but the launch has moved into a new phase.

The current job is to turn early outreach and visibility into a steady, understandable path from local awareness to qualified family visits, while learning which channels and messages produce real fit.

## Current status

As of June 3, 2026:

- the studio site has a family-visit-first funnel
- ongoing math support is introduced through tutoring and family visits, not a public pricing page
- the primary CTA is `Book a free family visit`
- analytics, ads, and tags are set up to track newsletter signups, Calendly signups, and August math camp interest
- 46 outbound email pings have been sent
- outreach has included PTA leaders from over 25 schools
- newsletter channels, parent Facebook groups, and other parent-community channels are being opened
- small Google Search campaigns are beginning as trial research, not as broad paid advertising

Signup segmentation:

- newsletter signup and August math camp interest submit to the same Buttondown endpoint
- the forms use distinct Buttondown tags and hidden metadata so camp-interest subscribers can be targeted separately from general newsletter subscribers
- a richer camp interest form may still be useful later if age group, availability, or child context becomes important before enrollment opens

## Strategic stance

The goal is not:

- to maximize traffic
- to build a giant marketing machine
- to fill the studio through low-fit volume
- to let Google Ads define the business by accident

The goal is:

- to generate qualified family visits
- to identify genuinely aligned families
- to learn which parent needs and search intents are real
- to build local trust through outreach, referrals, and useful visibility
- to preserve the studio's warmth, rigor, and relationship-based character while growing

The right framing is still `soft launch`, but the emphasis has shifted from initial setup to disciplined growth-and-learning.

## Primary funnel

Primary conversion path:

1. A local parent encounters The Number Garden through search, referral, outreach, social/community channel, email, or walk-by visibility.
2. They understand whether the studio may fit their child.
3. They book a free family visit.
4. The family visit clarifies fit, schedule, and next step.
5. If appropriate, Paul recommends an ongoing rhythm and enrollment next step.

Primary call to action:

- book a free family visit

Secondary calls to action:

- join the email list
- indicate interest in August math camp
- email with a specific question

## Audience priorities

Write primarily to the Seeking Parent.

This parent is looking for warmth, seriousness, and trust. They may not use the studio's internal language. They are more likely to search for practical phrases like math tutor, math help, math enrichment, math confidence, or summer math camp.

High-priority parent needs:

- a child needs more challenge
- a child has lost confidence in math
- school math feels too shallow, rushed, or worksheet-driven
- a family wants math to feel more alive
- a parent wants a trustworthy local adult to help them think about fit

High-priority learner signals:

- curious, puzzle-loving, or math-hungry students
- students who need confidence and foundations rebuilt with dignity
- children who would benefit from rich problems and calmer pacing

Avoid positioning that implies:

- test prep is the main offer
- speed and acceleration are the main value
- the studio is only for already-confident or gifted students
- the business is a generic tutoring marketplace

## Core offers

### Ongoing Math Support

Role:

- primary ongoing offer, introduced after a family visit

Primary next step:

- free family visit

The family visit is the main enrollment gateway. It gives the family a chance to see the space, meet Paul, talk about the child, and decide whether the studio feels like the right place to begin. Rhythm, schedule, cost, and access options are discussed after there is enough context for a real recommendation.

### August Math Camp

Role:

- seasonal offer and lead source

Primary next step:

- indicate camp interest

Camp interest should be measured separately from general newsletter interest. It can become a later path into ongoing support, but it should not be collapsed into the tutoring/family-visit funnel.

### Email List

Role:

- low-friction relationship building

Newsletter signup is useful, but it is a soft signal. It should not be counted the same way as a booked family visit or a camp-specific interest submission.

### 3. Google Search Ads

Role:

- targeted demand-capture experiment

Search ads should remain small, narrow, and research-oriented at first. This is not the broad paid advertising the original launch plan warned against. It is a controlled way to learn which search terms and parent needs produce qualified family visits or camp interest.

Summer approach:

- start with a small daily budget
- target a tight radius around the studio and/or selected nearby ZIP codes
- use phrase and exact match before broad match
- split ad groups by intent rather than putting all keywords together
- send family-visit traffic to `/family-visit/`
- send camp traffic to `/portland-math-camp/`
- review search terms frequently and add negative keywords aggressively

Initial ad group themes:

- Portland math tutoring
- math enrichment Portland
- math help for kids
- math confidence/support
- challenging math for kids
- August math camp Portland

Initial negative keyword themes:

- free worksheets
- online jobs
- tutor jobs
- college
- calculus help for adults
- test prep only
- SAT
- ACT
- printable
- curriculum PDF

Success criteria:

- qualified family visits, not clicks
- useful search-term learning
- camp interest from plausible local families
- landing-page conversion strong enough to justify continued spend

### 5. Email list

Role:

- owned audience and repeat exposure

Summer approach:

- use the list for studio openings, camp updates, and thoughtful parent-facing notes

### 6. Organic social content

Role:

- repeat local visibility, trust-building, and camp/tutoring conversion support

Summer approach:

- use Meta Business Suite as the day-to-day publishing home base
- keep a Markdown content calendar in the repo as the editorial source of truth
- aim for a sustainable weekly rhythm: 2 posts, 2 stories, and 1 reel
- make August math camp the primary conversion goal until camp demand is resolved
- keep tutoring/family-visit leads as the secondary conversion goal
- reuse existing quote graphics, logo motion assets, camp/event graphics, print collateral, and reel variations before creating new content
- preserve manual review and approval before anything publishes

Social content operating model:

- Planning: Markdown calendar entries define the week, format, campaign, goal, caption, CTA, destination URL, status, and asset references.
- Assets: source content, creative renderers, generated stills, video-intro exports, reel-family outputs, and print collateral stay in their natural repo locations.
- Publishing packets: approved items may be assembled into human-ready folders with final captions, platform notes, links, and chosen assets.
- Automation bridge: a future Make.com integration can read approved packets or an exported approval table, fetch hosted media, and prepare or publish to Facebook/Instagram.
- Publishing: keep actual Meta publishing manual until the workflow has proven itself; later automation should trigger only from an explicit approved status.

Future automation should support the workflow instead of replacing editorial judgment. The first useful automation target is packaging and handoff, not unsupervised posting.

## Measurement plan

### Primary metrics

Track weekly:

- family visits booked
- family visits completed
- enrollments from family visits
- camp interest submissions
- newsletter signups
- email questions
- outbound outreach pings sent
- responses to outreach
- paid-search spend
- paid-search conversions
- social posts/stories/reels shipped
- social-driven camp registrations or interest signals
- social-driven tutoring/family-visit inquiries
- source or campaign for each inquiry when available

### Quality metrics

Track manually when possible:

- student age
- family need: challenge, confidence, foundations, enrichment, camp, other
- geographic fit
- source: ad, organic search, newsletter, referral, local outreach, parent group, walk-by, other
- next step after first contact
- reason for non-fit or delay

### Useful ratios

Review at least monthly:

- visits booked per ad dollar
- family visits completed per family visits booked
- enrollments per family visit
- outreach responses per outbound ping
- qualified conversations per outreach channel
- camp interest submissions by source
- newsletter signups by source
- email questions by source

These ratios matter more than raw traffic.

## Conversion taxonomy

Current tracked events:

- `book_visit_click`: user clicks a family-visit CTA
- `calendly_popup_open`: Calendly popup opens
- `calendly_date_and_time_selected`: user chooses a time in Calendly
- `calendly_event_scheduled`: Calendly reports a scheduled event
- `calendly_booking_message_received`: site receives the Calendly scheduled-event message
- `book_appointment`: confirmation-page booking conversion
- `newsletter_signup_submit`: general newsletter form submit
- `camp_interest_signup_submit`: August math camp interest form submit
- `email_click`: user clicks an email link
- `booking_link_click`: user clicks the direct Calendly link
- `camp_interest_click`: user clicks to the camp interest section
- `camp_page_click`: user clicks to the camp page

Recommended conversion hierarchy:

- Primary Google Ads conversion: `book_appointment`
- Secondary Google Ads conversions: `camp_interest_signup_submit`, `newsletter_signup_submit`
- Diagnostic events: clicks, popup opens, date/time selected, email clicks

Do not optimize paid search primarily for clicks or newsletter signups unless family-visit volume is too low to learn from.

## Operating rhythm

Weekly:

- review outreach, spend, search terms, conversions, and inquiry quality
- add negative keywords
- record family-visit and camp-interest source notes
- decide one small copy, targeting, or channel adjustment

Monthly:

- compare channel performance
- review conversion ratios
- update the known-good keyword and negative keyword lists
- decide whether to increase, maintain, or reduce spend
- decide which outreach channels deserve more founder attention

End of summer:

- write a short results memo
- preserve what worked
- stop what produced low-fit interest
- choose Q4 marketing priorities from actual evidence
